Oroville Wright

The inventer of the aeroplane

Oroville's Wright Flyer achieved it's first successful flight in 1903 in Kitty Hawk, NC.
  • 1871 - Born in Dayton, Ohio
  • 1878 - Becomes fascinated with flight after his father brings home a small toy helicopter powered by rubber bands
  • 1889 - Drops out of high school before graduating; starts a printing business with his brother Wilbur Wright
  • 1892 - The Wright brothers open a bicycle sales and repair shop, which later funds their aviation experiments
  • 1896 - Inspired by the work and death of Otto Lilienthal, begins serious study of flight
  • 1899 - Writes to the Smithsonian Institution requesting information on aeronautics; begins designing aircraft
  • 1900 - Travels to Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, to test gliders due to its steady winds and soft sand
  • 1901 - Experiences major setbacks with glider performance; begins building a wind tunnel to test wing designs
  • 1902 - Develops a successful glider with a movable rudder, solving key control problems in flight
  • 1903 - Achieves the first powered, controlled airplane flight with the Wright Flyer at Kitty Hawk (December 17)
  • 1905 - Builds the first practical airplane capable of sustained flight
  • 1909 - Helps found the Wright Company to manufacture airplanes
  • 1912 - Brother Wilbur dies; Orville takes on greater responsibility for their aviation legacy
  • 1915 - Sells his interest in the Wright Company and withdraws from active business
  • 1915 to 1948 - Serves on advisory boards, including the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics, helping guide the future of aviation
  • 1948 - Dies at age 76 in Dayton, Ohio
